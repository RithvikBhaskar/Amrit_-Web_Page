ABOUT AMMA'S JOURNEY

Mātā Amritānandamayī Devi, affectionately known as Amma (which means “Mother”), is an Indian Hindu spiritual leader, guru, and humanitarian. She is revered by her followers as “the hugging saint” due to her compassionate embrace of millions of people. Here are some key details about her: Background and Early Life: Born as Sudhamani Idamannel on September 27, 1953, in Vallikavu, Kerala, India. She hails from a family of backward-caste Malayali fishermen. As a child, she witnessed poverty and suffering, which fueled her desire to help others. Compassionate Service: Amma collected food scraps from neighbors for her family’s cows and goats, encountering intense poverty. She shared her own food and clothing with those in need, despite her family’s disapproval. Amma spontaneously embraced people to comfort them, saying, “I don’t see anyone different from my own self.” Spiritual Leadership and Humanitarian Work: Founder of Mātā Amritānandamayī Math, an international charitable organization based in Amritapuri, Kerala. Chancellor of the multi-campus research university Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham. Honored by Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi for her significant contribution to the Clean India Campaign. First recipient of the Vishwaratna Puraskar (Gem of the World Award) by Hindu Parliament. Global Impact: Amma’s unconditional love transcends boundaries, and she has personally embraced over 40 million people. Her daily livestream from Amritapuri continues to inspire people worldwide.
